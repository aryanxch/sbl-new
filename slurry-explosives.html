OVERVIEW

The third generation of commercial explosives.

Slurry explosives — also known as water gels — are the third generation of commercial explosives, having largely replaced traditional explosives such as ANFO and gunpowder. A slurry explosive is a mixture of ammonium nitrate (or other nitrates) and fuel sensitizers, which may be a hydrocarbon, a combination of hydrocarbons, or aluminium.

They are made water-resistant through the use of gums, waxes, cross-linking agents or emulsifiers, which makes them well suited to wet boreholes in both surface and underground operations.

APPLICATION

How Slurry Explosives are used

Application, initiation and packaging differ by sub-class. Select the variant that matches your blast design.

Cap-Sensitive Cartridges Booster / Primer Charges

NEO PRIME SPECIAL, NEO BLAST SPECIAL

APPLICATION
  • Deep / long-hole blasting in opencast mines, quarrying and hill cutting
  • Good fragmentation with excellent water resistance
  • Can be used for plaster shooting / secondary blasting
HOW TO USE
  • Initiation with Detonator of No. 8 Strength and Detonating Cord
  • Air-gap sensitivity: 2 cm
PACKAGING
  • Cartridges packed in HDPE/LDPE film
  • Further packed in 25 kg corrugated boxes

Non-Cap-Sensitive Cartridges Column Charges

NEO BASE SPECIAL, NEO COL SPECIAL

APPLICATION
  • Deep / long-hole blasting in opencast mines, quarrying and hill cutting
  • Used as Column Charge with excellent water resistance
HOW TO USE
  • Non-cap-sensitive / non-aluminized explosive — must be used with a Cap-Sensitive explosive / Booster Charge
  • Air-gap sensitivity: 2 cm minimum (unconfined)
PACKAGING
  • Cartridges packed in HDPE/LDPE film
  • Further packed in 25 kg corrugated boxes
