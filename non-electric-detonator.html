OVERVIEW

Shock-tube initiation — flexibility & superior safety.

Non-electric detonators are widely used around the world. Their source of initiation is a shock wave that travels through a tube from one detonator to the next. Used in both commercial and military operations, the system consists of shock tubes connected to down-the-hole detonators and surface connectors, and can initiate explosive charges through chemical reactions such as rapid burning or detonation.

In addition to all the benefits of an electric detonator, they offer wide operational flexibility — making it easier to design large initiation sequences with, in theory, an unlimited number of delays — and greater safety, being insensitive to electricity, radio-frequency energy and electromagnetic radiation. This reduces the risk of accidental initiation from static electricity, stray currents and similar sources.

NEO™ DET is a non-electric initiating device for blasting that combines both a DTHD and an STLD in a single set (Twin Det). It consists of one coil of shock tube, crimped with a DTHD at one end and an STLD at the other, making it useful for both down-the-hole delay initiation and surface initiation within a blast. It is used in mines where the drilling and charge-loading patterns are fixed, reducing the shock-tube wastage that occurs when DTHD and STLD are used separately.

The STLD is housed in a suitable connector with provision to connect 6 shock tubes for initiation. Each NEO™ DET carries separate length-indicating and delay-time-indicating stickers for the DTHD and STLD for easy identification and handling. The delay detonator is of No. 8 strength. NEO™ DET provides unlimited delay periods and sequences for large-scale blasts, and is available to customer requirements in lengths of up to 50 metres.

Why our Non-Electric Detonators stand out.

  • ✓More safety — insensitivity to electricity, radio frequency energy, and electromagnetic radiation
  • ✓Less interruption — better safety from accidental initiation by static electricity, stray electrical currents, etc.
  • ✓STLD is used for surface hook-up — totally eliminates the air-blast noise
  • ✓Provides necessary relief delay to reduce the back-break and provides a good free face for the next drilling operation
  • ✓No disturbance of stemming column; no desensitisation of explosive column — higher explosive efficiency achieved
  • ✓Bottom initiation and ground vibration can be controlled
  • ✓Ultimate number of delays — particularly helpful in large blasts
  • ✓Leakage current in conductive ore bodies and watery hole will not cause misfire
